How long does an air conditioner last in Las Vegas?

Most homeowners are surprised to learn that their AC units can last 20+ years with proper care and maintenance. Based on real Las Vegas homeowner experiences, here's what you can expect:

Typical AC Lifespan in Las Vegas

  • 15-20 years is the average lifespan for well-maintained units
  • 20-25+ years is achievable with regular professional maintenance
  • 10-15 years for units with minimal upkeep or in challenging locations

Factors That Extend Your AC's Life

  • Regular maintenance - Twice-yearly professional service can add 5+ years
  • Timely filter changes - Especially important if you burn candles or have pets
  • Installation location - Units in direct sunlight may have shorter lifespans
  • Component replacement - Many units can be kept running with occasional part replacements (fan motors, capacitors, control boards)

When to Consider Replacement

  • When repair costs become excessive
  • When efficiency drops significantly (older 8 SEER vs. newer 16+ SEER units)
  • When comfort levels decrease despite repairs
  • When electricity bills climb despite maintenance

Cost-Saving Benefits of New Units

  • 20-30% lower energy bills with modern high-efficiency systems
  • Quieter operation - You'll barely notice newer systems running
  • More consistent cooling throughout your home
  • Better humidity control during monsoon season

Planning Ahead

Start budgeting for replacement when your unit reaches 15 years old. Setting aside $200-300 monthly will prepare you for the $10,000-$17,000 investment when the time comes.

Pro tip: Replace units during winter months when prices are typically lower and installation wait times are shorter.

Is it worth repairing a 20-year-old AC unit?

When your AC is 20+ years old but still working, it's a common dilemma: repair, replace, or wait? Here's what you need to know:

Generally, replacing a 20-year-old AC unit is more cost-effective than repairing it. While repairs might save money upfront, replacement usually offers better long-term value through energy savings, reliability, and comfort.

Key Factors to Consider

  • Age and Condition: At 20+ years, your AC has exceeded its expected 15-20 year lifespan
  • Refrigerant Type: R22 refrigerant (used in older units) is phased out, making repairs increasingly expensive
  • Energy Efficiency: New units are typically 30-40% more efficient than 20-year-old models
  • Repair Frequency: Frequent repairs signal it's time for replacement
  • Home Plans: Consider how long you'll stay in your home (though replacement often adds value)
